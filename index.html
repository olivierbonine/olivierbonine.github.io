Core Usability Laws

Foundational principles every UI audit and checkout flow must anchor on.

01 / Friction

Jakob's Law

Users spend most of their time on other sites. They expect your product to work similarly to what they already know. Minimize cognitive friction.

02 / Target Size

Fitts's Law

The time to acquire a target is a function of the distance to and size of the target. Crucial for thumb-zone targets on mobile checkout flows.

03 / Decisions

Hick's Law

The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices. Simplify complex menus to boost conversion rates.

04 / Framework

JTBD

"Jobs-to-be-Done." Users don't buy products; they hire them to make progress. Focus on the user's emotional and structural struggle.
